Personal Site

Branding / Web Design

The Problem

As a designer, I'm always evolving, learning new skills, and changing my visual style. I wanted a website that would better reflect who I am as a designer. First and foremost the site needed to be usable—not just something pretty to look at. It also needed to be flexible in terms of how I showcase my work.

The Solution

I decided to go with a single page site to keep navigation simple. I use AJAX to dynamically load the case studies in the work section. This allows the user to view case studies without leaving the homepage. Go ahead...scroll down. See, I told you. I thought it would be fun to show some of the different iterations throughout the process below.

As you can see below, these prototypes are not what the site looks like now. I actually built the site you see in the prototypes in its entirety. But it didn't feel right. In my effort to make the entire site feel like a mobile app, I sucked all the personality out of it. This is when I decided to go back to the drawing board. But I had the skeleton of the site already built, so I used it to help me rapid prototype some new ideas directly to HTML/CSS. The sketches above show a small turning point where I jumped from the original idea to the path that led me here.
